Hotteok

Description

This recipe comes from a friend who recommended this delectable pan fried stuffed pancake! Usually having chopped nuts and melted sugar in the center, Hotteok is a wonderfully comforting sweet treat.

Ingredients

  • Water (To desired texture)
  • 2 Tbs White Sugar
  • 2 Tsp Dry Yeast
  • 1/2 Tsp Kosher Salt
  • 1 Tbs Vegetable Oil
  • 2 1/2 Cups All Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 Cup Turbinado Sugar (Brown Sugar also works.)
  • 1 Tsp Cinnamon Powder
  • 2 Tbs Chopped Walnuts (Or desired nut.)

Steps

Dough

  1. First, to form our dough; place 1 cup of lukewarmwater into a bowl.
  2. Add white sugar, salt, yeast, and vegetable oil. Stir well.
  3. Add 2 cups of all purpose flour, mix (preferably) with a spoon.
  4. Allow dough to rise for 1 hour, with lid closed.
  5. After about an hour, the dough should have doubled in size. Knead to degass.
  6. Let dough rise for another 10-20 minutes.

Filling

  1. Mix Turbinado or brown sugar and cinnamon together.

Hotteok Production

  1. Knead dough to degass a third time.
  2. Dust around 1/2 cup of flour on surface
  3. Knead dough to develop a little gluten, then divide into eight portions.
  4. Take 1 dough portion, Flatten it, and add filling to preference in center, forming and sealing the dough to make a ball.
  5. Repeat until out of dough.
  6. Heat pan over medium low with a good amount of oil.
  7. Place ball on pan and wait 30 seconds.
  8. When dough ball bottom is light golden brown, press firmly with a spatula until it is about the size of a CD.
  9. Let cook until both sides are golden, or until center sugar has melted into a caramel.
  10. Cover and let steam if sides are getting too dark.
  11. Dig in!
